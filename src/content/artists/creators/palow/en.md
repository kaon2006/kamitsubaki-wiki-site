## Overview

PALOW. is an illustrator and character designer from Fukuoka Prefecture, active under the name PALOW. since 2011. He gained recognition in 2013 with the "Mushi Meka Shoujo" (Insect Mecha Girl) series, which was later used in a 2016 HAL vocational school television commercial.

Within KAMITSUBAKI STUDIO, PALOW.'s most significant contribution is serving as the character designer for KAF and Rim — two of the studio's foundational virtual singers. Their visual identities, from initial concepts to subsequent evolutions, are his work, playing a key role in establishing the early Kamitsubaki aesthetic.

## Role and Creative Position

PALOW.'s art is characterized by the fusion of complex geometric patterns, precise mechanical elements, and少女 imagery. His work often conveys a "self-contained world" quality — the figures seem to exist within their own universe, maintaining a微妙 distance from the outside world.

This aesthetic aligns closely with the virtual singer concepts of KAF and Rim: girl and machine, interior and exterior, intimacy and distance — PALOW.'s visual language captures the core tensions at the heart of the Kamitsubaki system.

## Activity History

- 2011: Began creative activities under the name PALOW.
- 2013: The "Mushi Meka Shoujo" series gained widespread attention.
- 2016: "Mushi Meka Shoujo" character designs were used in HAL vocational school TV commercials.
- Served as character designer, prop designer, and setting collaborator for the anime *Soutai Sekai*.
- Handled character design for the *League of Legends* 2019 animation PV.
- Created jacket illustrations, anime PV character designs, and costume designs for "Sanketsu Shoujo Sayuri."
- Designed the characters for KAF and Rim — his most important contribution to the Kamitsubaki ecosystem.
